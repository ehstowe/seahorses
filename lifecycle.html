Life Cycle

According to the Monterey Bay Aquarium, a seahorse's life cycle has six stages.

Stage 1: Adult

Adult seahorses stay around the same place for most of their life. They attach themselves to grasses or reefs and eat both plants and small animals.

Stage 2: Daily Greeting

A male and female seahorse meet each other.

Stage 3: Courtship

Eggs develop in the female and are passed to the male's "brood pouch" through a tube.

Stage 4: Pregnancy

The male seahorse's pouch grows and he provides nutrients for the eggs.

Stage5: Birth

The male seahorse pushes out many tiny seahorses.

Stage 6: Juvenile

The baby seahorses find a safe place to live and find food.
