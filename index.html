genus Hippocampus

Seahorse Facts

  • Seahorses can be as small as 2 centimeters and as large as 35 centimeters
  • They live mostly in salt water, often near coral reefs
  • They also may live in seagrass beds, estuaries, and mangroves
  • There are 32 different species of seahorses
  • Seahorses are omnivores
  • Seahorses are an endangered species

Seahorses are small aquatic animals that live in shallow salty waters in tropical and temperate areas. You can find them in sheltered areas such as seagrass beds, estuaries, coral reefs, and mangroves.
